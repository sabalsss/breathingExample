Breathe audibly through your nose or mouth on the inhale and exhale, and stay quiet during the holds. The detector listens for the broadband, noise-like sound of breath, so steady tones — humming, music, a fan's pitch, vowels — are largely ignored. Two honest limits remain: hissy speech sounds (s, f, sh, th) are physically the same turbulent noise as breath and will still register, and a single mic can sense breath activity vs. stillness but can't reliably tell an inhale from an exhale. For true direction sensing you'd need a chest-strap or dedicated breath sensor.

Why box breathing feels good — and works

  • 🫁Calms your nervous system. Slow, equal breaths nudge you out of fight-or-flight into rest-and-digest, easing heart rate and tension.
  • 🧠Sharpens focus. Counting and the held pauses give your attention a single anchor — which is why athletes and the military use it before high-pressure moments.
  • 😌Lowers stress. Paced breathing is linked in research to reduced anxiety and lower stress hormones.
  • 😴Helps you wind down. Downshifting your arousal makes it a great reset before sleep, or right after a hard moment.
  • ⚡Works fast, costs nothing. A few rounds can change how you feel — portable, private, and it needs nothing but you.

Box breathing (a.k.a. four-square breathing) is a simple, evidence-informed way to self-regulate, used by Navy SEALs, athletes, and clinicians. It isn't a medical treatment — if you ever feel lightheaded, just return to normal breathing. Come back daily to keep your streak alive. 🔥
