Breathe audibly through your nose or mouth on the inhale and exhale, and stay quiet during the holds. The detector listens for the broadband, noise-like sound of breath, so steady tones — humming, music, a fan's pitch, vowels — are largely ignored. Two honest limits remain: hissy speech sounds (s, f, sh, th) are physically the same turbulent noise as breath and will still register, and a single mic can sense breath activity vs. stillness but can't reliably tell an inhale from an exhale. For true direction sensing you'd need a chest-strap or dedicated breath sensor.
